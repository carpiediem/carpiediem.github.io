At Emotics, I built a suite of tools to collect data, submit and process it, and display it to stakeholders. This started with a modularized JavaScript SDK, which allowed different features to be easily enabled, disable, and configured in different integrations. The SDK could be embedded directly into web pages or packaged into e-learning content, like SCORM modules. The SCORM injected a new menu into the existing content to present terms and conditions to the learner and request that they agree to have the learning session monitored. After that it would capture snapshots from the webcam and analyze the imagery for authentication and attention tracking.

No imagery left the learners' laptops. Instead our artificial intelligence ran in their browser and sent only its results to the Emotics API. This was built on Express.js and deployed with AWS Elastic Beanstalk to ensure scalability and reliable service. This utilized a PostreSQL database cluster on RDS for storage.

After some post-processing to summarize each session and optimize data schemas for reporting, our analytics console front-end could be used to review the collected data and the insights our system provided. Compiance managers could identify potential problems with ignored sessions or cheating and learning management teams could identify exactly which elements of their content were succeeding and which were putting learners to sleep. The console was built as a React.js single-page app using material design standards.
