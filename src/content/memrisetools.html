When I started studying Mandarin Chinese, I found a relatively new learning website named Memrise.com. At its core, Memrise is a set of flashcards that help you remember facts of all types, from animal identification to history trivia. Most of their content, however, focuses on learning foreign languages, as I was. Flash cards are presented not in a strict order, but algorithmically- the words that Memrise thinks you should prioritize.

Courses are mostly user-curated (I made a few to match the vocabulary in my courses' textbooks). Each "flashcard" is augmented with what the website calls mems (a play on memes) that help learners remember individual facts using visual, audio, or mnemonic cues.

Although the platform grew in user-based content, it was poorly maintained for quite awhile. User forums were identifying easy-to-do improvements, but Memrise did not have the manpower to implement them. I joined a few other volunteers in writing user scripts to make temporary fixes and improvements to the site. Most of these no longer work, since Memrise has since been through a few redesigns, but they were good practice in reverse-engineering an existing app.
